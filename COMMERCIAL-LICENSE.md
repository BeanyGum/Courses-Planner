# Commercial & Organizational Licensing

Course Plan is dual-licensed:

- **Personal Use** — free, for a single individual using it for their own
  purposes. See [LICENSE](LICENSE) for the exact terms.
- **Everyone else** — requires a paid Commercial License Agreement.

## Who needs a commercial license?

Short version: if you're not one person using this for yourself, you
probably need one. This includes, without limitation:

- **Universities and schools** — deploying Course Plan for students to
  use (on a university website, student dashboard, institutional server,
  or similar), even if no one is charged and even for a pilot or trial.
- **Businesses** — using Course Plan internally, as part of a product,
  or in any commercial offering.
- **Nonprofits and government bodies** — any organizational use, paid or
  unpaid.
- **Anyone reselling, rebranding, or redistributing** Course Plan or a
  modified version of it.
- **Anyone hosting Course Plan as a service** for others to use.

A student using their own copy on their own laptop for their own degree
plan does **not** need a commercial license. A professor or IT
department installing it on a university server for students to use
**does**, even if it's free to those students.

## Why require this even for unpaid/educational deployments?

Institutional deployment (SSO integration, per-student data storage,
uptime, support expectations, liability) is a materially different
relationship than someone running a copy on their own machine. A
commercial agreement lets both sides set clear terms — scope of use,
data handling, support, duration — rather than relying on informal
assumptions.

## How to request a commercial license

Email **hassaneinanasm@outlook.com** with:

1. Who you are and what organization you represent.
2. How you intend to use Course Plan (e.g. number of students, hosting
   environment, whether it'll integrate with other systems like SSO or
   a student dashboard).
3. Timeline (e.g. pilot, semester rollout, ongoing).

Terms (including any fee, which may be $0 for some arrangements at the
Licensor's discretion) are negotiated case by case.
